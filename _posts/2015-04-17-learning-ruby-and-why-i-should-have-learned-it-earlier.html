\n\nThe Internet has said it over and over. Did I listen? Not right away. The almighty Interwebs have said “LEARN RUBY FIRST!” Nah, I do things my way, I do what I want. I should have listened.\r\n\r\nI spent the past week and a half sitting down, learning the basics of Ruby, and guess what. Rails makes SO MUCH MORE SENSE NOW! So I should have listened to the wise words of those who have come before me.\r\n\r\nActually, I think that attempting to learn Rails before Ruby helped some. I understood some of the concepts, but then after diving deep into Ruby it all started to click.\r\n\r\nThe use of hashes, arrays, classes, and all the verbiage that came with Ruby became a snowball in my mind. Once the ball began rolling I was able to comprehend even more. I went back through some of my projects that I had done originally and just read my code.\r\n\r\nIt sounds like a very noob-ish mistake, but I should have learned Ruby right from the beginning. I’m a stubborn guy though sometimes, and like I said, I wanted to do what I wanted to do. Now, I will say that I am not a Ruby expert per say, but I think that I have enough of the basics down that I can go look at Ruby-doc and pick my way through them to get what I want.\r\n\r\nMy next project for myself will be going through and refactoring my code that is just messy and not clean.
